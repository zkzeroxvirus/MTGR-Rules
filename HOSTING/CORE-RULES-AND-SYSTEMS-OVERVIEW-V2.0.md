# MTG Roguelike — Core Rules & Systems (V2.0)
Quick operational overview for players and hosts.

---

## 1. Resources

There are two primary resources in MTG Roguelike.

Essence:
- Long-term progression currency.
- Persists between sessions.
- Used for pre-run and post-run progression purchases.

XP:
- In-run currency.
- Spent during the current run (Town and Wanderers).
- Lost when the run ends.

Guideline:
- Spend XP for immediate run power.
- Spend Essence for long-term account growth.

---

## 2. Pre-Game Setup Process

Players complete these steps before Encounter 1.

1. Commander Selection
- Generate 5 legal commanders.
- Choose 1.
- Commander rerolls can come from specific effects.

2. Nonland Deck Generation
- Generate one shared 100-card nonland pool within commander color identity.
- Draft 22 nonland cards from that pool.
- The 100-card pool is not rerolled as a whole.
- If a specific generated card is invalid/illegal, replace only that card.

3. Lands
- Add 16 total lands.
- Dual land allocation scales by color count:
- 2 colors: +2 duals
- 3 colors: +4 duals
- 4 colors: +6 duals
- 5 colors: +8 duals
- Remaining lands are basic.

4. Free Choice Card
- Add 1 free choice card.
- Must follow color identity.
- Cannot be Sol Ring.
- Must be Commander legal.

5. Finalize
- Apply persistent progression effects and legal pregame choices.
- Confirm deck is legal at Commander + 39.
- Resolve Event or Trinket pre-first-encounter step.

---

## 3. Core Gameplay Loop

After deckbuilding, loop this structure until Crypt.

1. Encounter
- Host resolves encounter setup.
- Both sides roll d20.
- High roll chooses turn order.

2. Combat Resolution
- If players win: resolve rewards.
- If players lose: use failed-encounter branch.

3. Rewards (Victory Only)
- Resolve in strict order:
1. XP
2. Cash Out
3. Loot Pool

4. Path Choice (Victory Only)
- Return to Town, or
- Stay Out

5. Event Resolution
- After Town or Stay Out, resolve Event(s), then proceed.

Pre-Encounter-1 step:
- Resolve Event or Trinket (one of the two unless an explicit rule says otherwise).

---

## 4. Death, Victory, And Failure

Player death:
- Death is not permanent.
- Player returns in later encounter flow.
- No XP penalty in base rules.

Encounter failure:
- No XP from that encounter.
- No Cash Out or Loot Pool.
- Encounter stage is not replayed.
- Advance directly to next encounter stage.
- Skip Town/Stay Out and Events for that stage.
- Full HP reset still applies before next encounter.

Run failure condition:
- The run ends only if the party fails to defeat the Crypt.

---

## 5. Town vs Stay Out

Town path:
- All players fully heal.
- Resolve Town building usage.
- Building-limited uses reset each Town visit.
- Resolve 1 Event, then proceed.

Stay Out path (resolve in order):
1. Gain Stay Out XP
2. Gain 2 Mystery Packs per player
3. Resolve Supply Drop
4. Resolve Wanderer
5. Resolve Event(s)

Stay Out scaling:
- XP: +10 base per player, then +5 per consecutive Stay Out.
- Events: 2 on first Stay Out, then +1 event for each additional consecutive Stay Out.
- Both scale tracks reset when the party returns to Town.

Important:
- Stay Out uses full HP reset, not half-heal.

---

## 6. Host Scaling

Encounter scaling increases as the run advances.

What scales:
- Host HP by player count and encounter stage.
- Affix tier count by encounter stage and mode.
- Doom and Authority by player count.

Modes:
- Standard mode: 3 encounters, then Crypt (no Tier 4 affixes).
- Variant mode: 4 encounters, then Crypt (Tier 4 affixes used).

---

## 7. Affixes

Affixes increase encounter power and bonus XP.

Affix bonus XP by tier:
- Tier 1: +5 XP
- Tier 2: +10 XP
- Tier 3: +15 XP
- Tier 4: +20 XP

Total encounter XP on victory:
- Base XP (mode/stage) + Affix bonus XP + encounter-specific bonus XP.

---

## 8. Crypt Progression

Crypt is the final encounter.

Crypt notes:
- Significantly harder than prior stages.
- Host always goes first.

On Crypt victory:
- Run is completed.
- Players receive Essence rewards, Crypt Buff rewards, and additional progression rewards as defined by active systems.

---

## 9. Turn Structure

Party turn (shared):
- Untap
- Upkeep
- Draw
- Main
- Combat
- Main 2
- End step

Host turn:
- Host plays normally.

Rules:
- Only the controller of an extra-turn effect takes that extra turn unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

## 10. Loot And Cash Outs

Encounter victory grants:
- XP
- Cash Out choices
- Loot Pool cards

Cash Out rules:
- Host reveals cash outs equal to player count.
- Each player chooses one unless otherwise specified.
- Cash Outs are tradable and retained until used.

Loot Pool rules:
- Reveal cards from defeated encounter deck equal to player count.
- Reroll basic lands.
- Place into shared pool for selection.

---

## 11. System Summary

Core strategic loop:
- Encounter difficulty scales over time.
- Rewards convert wins into growth.
- Town provides controlled upgrades.
- Stay Out provides higher variance and scaling upside.

Players must balance:
- Survival
- XP/Essence management
- Risk vs control decisions
- Deck legality while adapting between stages
